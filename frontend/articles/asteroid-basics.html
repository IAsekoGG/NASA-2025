What is a meteorite?

Asteroids are rocky or metallic objects smaller than a planet that orbit the Sun. Most asteroids in our Solar System are located in the main asteroid belt, the region between Mars and Jupiter.

When a fragment breaks off an asteroid, in space it is called a meteoroid.

NASA tracks all objects larger than 140 meters that could approach Earth.
A meteoroid that enters the atmosphere flares as a meteor; and if it survives the fall and reaches the ground, it becomes a meteorite.

Fact: Each year Earth accumulates from 20,000 to 40,000 tons of cosmic dust, about 10% of which survives entry into the atmosphere, becoming micrometeorites.
